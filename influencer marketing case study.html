Influencer Marketing App That Empowers Authentic Collaboration

Designing an Influencer Marketing App That Empowers Authentic Collaboration

At our core, we wanted to solve a pain that both brands and influencers face—finding each other, working together seamlessly, and ensuring that every part of the collaboration journey feels smooth, transparent, and rewarding.

My Role
Research & Design
Timeline
2 months

The Proposal

A match-making engine:

Think Bumble meets LinkedIn for influencers & brands

Creator monetization tools:

Thrift stores, workshops, and affiliate tools—all in-app

Collaboration dashboard:

From outreach to final content, everything is trackable

Emotional UX:

Collaborative, clear, and made to feel like a real partnership

UX Challenges

Making the collaboration process feel less like "task management" and more like a creative partnership

Balancing the needs of two very different personas (influencer vs. brand manager)

Capturing key campaign data without making it feel like a form-filling chore

Standing out in a space crowded with talent databases, but poor in creative tools

Audience & Timing

For Influencers: Micro to mid-tier creators looking to monetize meaningfully, not just transact

For Brands: D2C and mid-sized marketing teams wanting better reach, deeper engagement, and less operational chaos.

Step 1: Empathy & Discovery

We started with honest conversations. 15 influencers. 10 brand marketers.
No sales pitch—just understanding what made or broke a campaign.
Insights We Uncovered

Trust = Everything.

Creators hate being treated like ad slots. Brands struggle to know who’s actually reliable.

End-to-End Visibility Needed

From product delivery to final post, both sides wanted a single place to track every step.

Creators Want More Than Campaigns

Thrifting preloved pieces, hosting niche workshops, affiliate links—this was the future of creator monetization.

Competitor Deep Dive

We studied over 12 platforms—Aspire, Upfluence, Collabstr, even Instagram’s built-in tools. Common Gaps

  • The timing was also key—creator economy fatigue was real. Influencers were overwhelmed by cold pitches, and brands were drowning in spreadsheets and half-managed partnerships. We aimed to be a fresh, simple, and effective alternative.
  • We saw a chance to be more personal, more dynamic, and genuinely useful beyond just brief → post.

Designing the Match + Collab Flow

Social Profile & Rate Card Submission

As part of the verification process, influencers are first required to connect their social media profiles. They are also asked to submit their rate card by filling out the provided form, ensuring brands have transparent access to their pricing information.

Stronger Verification

In the next step, for stronger verification, influencers are required to provide their email, ID proof, mobile number, profile picture, bank account details, along with their social media profile (already connected in the previous step)."

Refactoring

We used NLP to parse briefs and suggest creators aligned in tone, not just category. Example: “Looking for quirky, nostalgic creators” Finds profiles tagged with “90s vibes”, “vintage edits”, etc.

New brands create campaigns by filling out a form. For returning brands, the system saves data from previous campaigns and generates templates that can be tweaked and reused — making campaign creation faster and easier

Finding Influencers

Brands can easily search and discover influencers using advanced filter options.

Trusted Decisions

Before collaborating, brands can review an influencer’s profile, rate card, past campaigns, and feedback from other brands — helping them build trust and make informed decisions

Content Drafts + Approvals

The entire campaign is tracked from both ends — by influencers and brands — ensuring a smooth and hassle-free process.

Finalize Campaign

Before making a payment, brands can review all posted content, stories, and campaign performance in one place. Once satisfied, they complete the payment to the influencer to finalize the deal.

Monetization for Creators

Most platforms stop at
campaigns. We didn’t.

  • We added tools for:
    Thrift Store Pages: Creators can sell preloved items right in-app (fashion, props, gear)
  • Workshop Hosting: Run paid virtual sessions (e.g., “How I shoot reels with no tripod”)
  • Affiliate Hub: Share products with embedded affiliate tracking
  • This made the app a daily tool, not just a campaign inbox.

Email & Engagement Strategy

Our aim wasn’t just to onboard users—but to keep them.

Campaign Tracker Emails: Summary of all active collabs + pending actions

New Match Alerts: Weekly digest of potential collabs

Workshop Invites & Community Spotlights: Celebrate top creators, showcase learning sessions
